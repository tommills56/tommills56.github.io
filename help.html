The EnglishMaster® provides users with either FREE usage mode or CHARGE usage mode. With FREE usage mode, a user can only access a very limited test content resource with limited time and functionality. And with CHARGE usage mode, a user can fully use all functionality and access all test content resources, which are continuously updated. When users login the system, they are in the FREE mode by default.

Currently we provide user with four usage modes: Free, Economic, BestChoice, and Unlimited. All modes except Free are CHARGE modes.

All usage time within EnglishMaster is monthly based. The usage time in CHARGE mode is called charge time and the usage time in FREE mode called free time. Different usage time is counted separately.

When you click "CHANGE MODE" button, the system is changed to another usage mode and the new title is displayed in the main window of the application, which clearly indicates the new usage mode.

Click "ACCOUNT" button and log in to see a detailed list of all available usage modes. Then you can select and buy a suitable usage mode and enjoy your selection.

To measure your critical thinking and analytical writing skills. It assesses your ability to articulate and support complex ideas, analyze an argument, and sustain a focused and coherent discussion.

You will have 45 minutes to plan and compose a response that presents your perspective on the topic you selected. You will have a choice between two Issue topics. You are free to accept, reject, or qualify the claim made in the topic, as long as the ideas you present are clearly relevant to the topic you select. Support your views with reasons and examples drawn from each area as your reading, experience, observations, or academic studies.

To measure your critical thinking and analytical writing skills. It assesses your ability to articulate and support complex ideas, analyze an argument, and sustain a focused and coherent discussion.

You will have 30 minutes to plan and write a critique of an argument presented in the form of a short passage.

Analyze the line of reasoning in the argument. Be sure to consider what, if any, questionable assumptions underlie the thinking and, if evidence is cited, how well it supports the conclusion.

You can also discuss what sort of evidence would strengthen or refute the argument, what changes in the argument would make it more logically sound, and what additional information might help you better evaluate its conclusion. Note that you are NOT being asked to present your view on the subject.
